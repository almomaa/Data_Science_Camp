Good sync, bad sync

The very same maths — many oscillators pulling together — can keep you alive or put you in danger. It all depends on how much they sync.

healthy · good

Heartbeat

Pacemaker cells sync just enough to beat as one, steady and strong. This synchronization is literally keeping you alive.

seizure · dangerous

Brain seizure

When far too many neurons fire in lock-step at once, the result is an epileptic seizure. Here, too much sync is the illness.

crowd · for fun

Applause

An audience drifts from random clapping into a rhythmic beat — spontaneous sync you can hear, harmless and delightful.
